Symbol Of Copyright


Circled C symbol “©” is the Designated copyright symbol as per the Copyright Registration Act, 1957 . This circled C can be used for all creative works except sound production, which is denoted by a circled P mark. This mark indicates that the creation is officially registered and the same design used by any third party is illegal & punishable.

Process


Step 1: Prepare and submit application

Step 2: Diary number will be issued by the registrar.

Step 3: Mandatory waiting of 30 days for objection.

Step 4: After an objection filed, there are two possibilities, which are as follows: In case if no objections are raised, the examiner goes ahead to review and scrutinizes the application to find any discrepancy.

  • If there is no fault and all the essential documents provided along with the application, it is a case of zero discrepancies. In this case, the applicant is allowed to go forward with the next step.
  • In case some discrepancies are found, a letter of discrepancy sent to the applicant—a hearing conducted by the registrar based upon his reply. Once the discrepancy is resolved, the applicant is allowed to move forward to the next step.

In case objections are raised by someone against the applicant, letters are sent out to both parties and the registrar calls for a hearing.


  • Upon hearing if the objection gets rejected, the application goes ahead for scrutiny, and the procedure for discrepancy mentioned above is followed.
  • In case the objection is not clarified, or discrepancy is not resolved, the application is rejected and a rejection letter sent to the applicant. For such applicants, the copyright registration procedure ends here.

Step 5:The final process termed as registration. The process registration of Copyright completes when the applicant is issued the Extracts of the Register of Copyrights (ROC).
