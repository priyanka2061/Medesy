What Is A Partnership Firm ?

A partnership is a type of business organization in which two or more people come together to run and operate a Firm in accordance with the conditions and purposes set out in the partnership deed.


If there is only one promoter/founder, One Person Company (OPC) is the best way to start a company. Partnership deed is an agreement between the partners in which rights, duties, profits shares and other obligations of each partner is mentioned.


A Partnership is easy to form, and the compliance is minimal as compared to companies.

An Overview


Indian Partnership Act, 1932 governs the partnerships. Registration of partnership firm is optional and at the discretion of the partners.
By introducing a limited liability partnership (LLP), one may opine that Partnership firms has lost relevance because partners have unlimited liability, which means they are personally liable for the debts of the business which is not in case of LLP.
However, Partnership Firms are preferred since there are low costs of setting up the business and minimal compliance requirements, which makes it a sensible option for home/closed family businesses that are unlikely to take on any debt. Registration with Registrar of firms (ROF) is optional for Partnerships but advisable.

Advantages

  • Minimum Compliance
      • Audit is not compulsory for a partnership firm. Whereas, LLPs and companies has to file yearly returns with the MCA- ROC which is not the case in a partnership firm.

  • Easy to form and Close
      Process to form a partnership firm is quite simple. Finding a Firm does not require any complicated legal process. Firm registration is not mandatory requirement. Firm can also easily register if required. You can easily close a Firm..
  • Comparatively Economical
      The partnership is much cheaper to start than an LLP. Even in the long run, it will still work cheaply since the compliance requirements are minimal..

  • No minimum initial Capital Contribution
      There is no minimum capital contribution requirement in case of a partnership firm. It can be started with a capital of as low as Rs. 10,000.

  • Sharing Risks
      The partnership organization ensures various financial risks by allowing more than one person to share the risk because profit and loss are shared by different partners.

  • Better Tax Planning
      The partnership firm is a separate legal entity and its tax is calculated separately. The interest, salaries, bonuses, commissions or remuneration paid to the partner are granted to the Partnership Firm as a deduction. It is a legal tool for tax planning.

  • Raising of Funds
      Financial Institutions view a partnership more credible while lending loans and giving credit facilities instead of a proprietorship firm. Multiple partners can make for more feasible contributions.

Disadvantages

  • Number of partners
      • The Partnership Firm has a maximum number of 100 members.

  • Unlimited liability
      Each partner is jointly and severally responsible for the firms debts. A spouse's dishonesty can devastate an entire firm and cause serious difficulties for everyone.

  • Lack of perpetual succession
      A partnership firm does not have a separate legal existence of its own. It is dissolved upon the death of a partner or upon separation between them.

  • Limited source of External funds
      Ownership structure does not allow investment by angel investors, venture capitalists or private equity firms. Banks / financial institutions also prefer to lend to companies rather than partnership firms, as companies are independent entities and regulatory requirements for corporate financial reporting make the company more transparent and structured.

  • Transferability of Interest
      Transfer of interest from one partner to an outsider is difficult and requires unanimous acceptance from all other existing partners. Therefore, an investment in a partnership business is an illiquid asset.

Difference Between Partnership Firm & LLP?



LLP Partnership Firm
Cost of Formation
Around 6k-8k Around 3k-4K
Registered Under
Ministry of Corporate Affairs, Central Government Registrar of Firms, Controlled by the respective State Government in which the firm is registered.
Liability of partner’s
Partners are not responsible for other partner’s misconduct One partner is not responsible or liable for another partner’s misconduct or negligence
No. of Partners
Minimum 2 Maximum unlimited Minimum 2 Maximum 100
Perpetual Succession
In case of a LLP, if the number of Partners reduces below 2, the sole Partner can still find a new Partner to fill the position without dissolution of the LLP. In case of a Partnership Firm, if the number of partners at any time reduces below the mandatory minimum of 2 due to death, incapacitation or resignation of a Partner, the partnership firm would stand dissolved.

Requisite Documents


    Even after the foundation, the Partnership Firm can be registered at any time as per Section 58 of the Indian Partnership Act. A Partnership Firm is registered through the Registrar of Firms (ROF).
  • Passport size Photograph, Pan Card and ID proof ( Driving License/Aadhaar Card/Passport/ Voter ID) of all partners.
  • Original copy of partnership deed signed by all partners.
  • Stamp Paper (for payment of stamp duty on total initial capital contribution of firm).
  • Stamp Paper of Rs.100/- (For firm registration certificate).
  • Regional Language translation of Deed translated by Professional Translator with Notary.
  • Form “A” duly signed by all partners and notarised (Need to create Firm login on ROF portal).
  • Affidavit declaring intention to become partner - Require when Firm apply for IFC code.
  • Authority letter of Firm to authorised Professionals to complete Firm registration process.
  • Covering letter of Professionals. Require at the time of submission of documents.
  • Rental or lease agreement or Electricity Bill or any other utility bill of the property/campus on which the Business is set.


Compliance Required By Partnership Firms


    Accounting & Book Keeping

    It is always recommended to log the Partnership Firms business in cash or on a periodic basis to determine if these companies are making a profit..

    Tax Audit

    Partnership Firm are not required by law to maintain their accounts unless the Firms income for the financial year exceeds Rs. 1 Cr. and the total income from the profession exceeds Rs. 50 Lakh. In both cases, auditing partners under the 1961 Income Tax Act is mandatory.

    Income Tax Returns

    Income tax returns must be submitted by all affiliated companies with taxable income.

    GST Registrations And Returns

    If your annual turnover exceeds the prescribed amount, you must register for GST. If you run an online business (sold via Amazon, Flipkart, etc.) you would be required to get a GST number based on the tax law for goods and services. Once you have registered under GST then you are required to file monthly or quarterly details of sale and purchases.

    MSME Registration

    You can register as a small business (SME) according to the MSME Act. Applications can be submitted electronically. Registration as an SME is not required, but can be very beneficial, especially if you get a business loan. It is also very helpful to get a Udyog Aadhaar registration / Udyam registration.
